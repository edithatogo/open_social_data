# Accessible Guide to the New Zealand Health Survey Annual Update

**Source:** New Zealand Ministry of Health  
**Guide Updated:** 2026-06-23

## What is This Data About?

This data summarises selected health results from the New Zealand Health Survey. It can include indicators about health behaviours, health status, access to care, and other public health topics.

## Why is This Data Important?

Health survey results help people understand broad patterns in population health and differences between groups. They can inform public health planning, service design, and community reporting.

## How Can You Use This Data?

You might compare an indicator across age groups or regions, or look at whether a result has changed between survey years. Use confidence intervals and source flags when deciding whether a difference is meaningful.

## Key Things to Know Before Using This Data

Survey estimates have uncertainty. Small groups can have wider confidence intervals, and some values may be suppressed or flagged. Avoid ranking communities or groups without checking uncertainty and context.
