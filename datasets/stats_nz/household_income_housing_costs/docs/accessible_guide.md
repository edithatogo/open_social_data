# Accessible Guide to Household Income and Housing-Cost Statistics

**Source:** Stats NZ  
**Guide Updated:** 2026-06-23

## What is This Data About?

This data helps explain household income, housing costs, and related living-standard measures in New Zealand. It can show how income and housing-cost measures differ by household type, tenure, region, or population group, depending on the selected source table.

## Why is This Data Important?

Income and housing costs affect people's ability to meet basic needs, participate in communities, and manage financial stress. These statistics are useful for housing policy, poverty analysis, cost-of-living reporting, and local planning.

## How Can You Use This Data?

You might compare housing-cost ratios for renters and homeowners, or look at how household incomes differ across groups. Always check whether a value is a dollar amount, a percentage, or a ratio before comparing it.

## Key Things to Know Before Using This Data

Survey estimates are based on sampled households, so small subgroups can have more uncertainty. Definitions also matter: disposable income, equivalised income, and gross income are not interchangeable. Use the official source notes before drawing conclusions about inequality or hardship.
